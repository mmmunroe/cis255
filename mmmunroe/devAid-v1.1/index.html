What is TEDxSVSU?

TEDxSVSU is an independently organized TED-style event, a full day of local, national and international speakers, music and artistic presenters, and also sponsors and exhibitors who showcase novel displays and activities. The target audience is SVSU and the surrounding community, especially students, parents, families, staff, faculty, and administration. The event attracts educated people throughout mid-Michigan and beyond. Anyone interested in TED-style talks is welcome to attend TEDxSVSU. Organizers and leaders of the event include SVSU students, faculty and staff. The project is supported by the SVSU Foundation Research Grant Program.

What is TED?

TED is a nonprofit devoted to spreading ideas, usually in the form of short, powerful talks (18 minutes or less). TED began in 1984 as a conference where Technology, Entertainment and Design converged, and today covers almost all topics from science to business to global issues in more than 100 languages. Meanwhile, independently run TEDx events help share ideas in communities around the world.

What is TEDx?

TEDx was created in the spirit of TED's mission, "ideas worth spreading." It supports independent organizers who want to create a TED-like event in their own community.

Location

TEDxSVSU will be held at Saginaw Valley State University in Curtis Hall and the conference will be in Banquet Rooms A & B located on the second floor. It can be reached by stairs or elevator located by the SVSU Bookstore with signs and directions posted at entryways. Parking is available in front of Curtis Hall in lots J1 and J2.

Speakers and Exhibits

Judge Darnell Jackson

Topic: The Value of Human Worth

Darnell Jackson is a Circuit Court Judge, informational speaker and nationwide presenter for the National Judicial Institute on Domestic Violence. He is the author of the acclaimed book The Steps of A Good Man, which provides practical and proven methods for dealing with the challenges and setbacks of life. His philosophy is, "I have an obligation to those who hear me speak to provide them with something of significance that they didn't have before."

Dr. Tracy Weber

Topic: Horse Wisdom for the Human World

Tracy Weber, Ph.D. is a recognized leader in equine-assisted learning (EAL), a university professor, an author, and entrepreneur. In addition to her state-of-the art facility in Michigan, she has facilitated EAL learning programs throughout the world, including Dubai, Croatia, Puerto Rico and several states in the US. Her engaging presentation will prompt spirited dialogue about humans becoming more horse-like.

Franz Schneider

Topic: You hired an employee, but a human being showed up instead

Franz Schneider, CEO Humantech, is a prominent authority on what organizations must do to get the best from their people. His client list is comprised of the world's most influential as well as profitable companies. A speaker at literally thousands of events, his humor and talent to make the complex usable has warmed audiences on six continents; Antarctica is still on his bucket list. Franz's TEDx presentation will focus on three criteria every organization must embrace to close the gap between "what people can do if they are inspired and the least they do not to get fired."

What are Exhibits?

Exhibits are booth-style displays of novel local technology, entertainment and design implementations. Exhibitors can include sponsoring corporations, students or local technologists or artisans. The purpose of exhibits is to give the audience a chance to see and experience things that they would not normally have exposure to either in daily life or even in college classes.
